Normal zone (1 pt)
Hot zone (2 pts)
The 6×6 grid has 9 marked zones — 6 normal zones worth 1 point each and 3 hot zones worth 2 points each. Both teams start in opposite corners with 4 tokens. All other cells are neutral movement space.

Match structure
Setup
30 sec
Live play
5 min
Tally
30 sec
How to play
1
Teams alternate turns — on your turn, move one token one square (orthogonal only)
No diagonal moves. A token cannot move onto a cell occupied by your own token.
2
A zone is claimed when your token enters it
Place a small marker (a coin flat on the table) to show ownership. The zone is yours until the opponent enters it.
3
When an opponent enters your claimed zone, it flips to them
Zones change hands freely throughout the game. Only the state at time-up matters.
4
A zone is locked if you have 2 or more of your tokens inside it simultaneously
A locked zone cannot be flipped — opponent tokens entering it are blocked and must move away next turn. This is the core defensive mechanic.
5
When the 5-minute timer ends, count all claimed zones
Each normal zone = 1 pt. Each hot zone = 2 pts. Highest score wins the match.
The lock mechanic is the key decision: committing 2 tokens to lock one zone leaves only 2 tokens to contest the remaining 8 zones. Hold too tight and you lose the board. Spread too thin and you can't lock anything.
